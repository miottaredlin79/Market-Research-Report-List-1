<p><ul><li>Cryoablation</li><li>High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound (HIFU)</li><li>Laser Interstitial Thermal Therapy (LiTT)</li><li>Microwave Ablation (MWA)</li><li>Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA)</li><li>Irreversible Electroporation (Ire)</li><li>Non-Thermal Ablation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Cancer ablation devices encompass various technologies used to destroy tumor cells. Cryoablation employs extreme cold to freeze and eliminate tissues. High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound (HIFU) uses focused ultrasound waves to heat and destroy tumors non-invasively. Laser Interstitial Thermal Therapy (LiTT) involves laser light for targeted heating. Microwave Ablation (MWA) utilizes microwave energy for rapid tumor destruction. Radiofrequency Ablation (RFA) applies radio waves to generate heat. Irreversible Electroporation (IRE) introduces electrical pulses to disrupt cell membranes. Non-Thermal Ablation refers to techniques that do not rely on temperature changes to ablate tissues.</p></p>

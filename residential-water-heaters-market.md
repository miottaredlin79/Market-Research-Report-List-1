<p><p>The residential water heater market comprises two main types: storage tank and tankless heaters. Storage tank heaters, the most common, consist of insulated tanks that preheat and store water for immediate use, offering reliability for households with consistent hot water demands. In contrast, tankless heaters provide on-demand hot water, heating it as needed without storage, which enhances energy efficiency and reduces space requirements. Both types cater to varying consumer needs, energy efficiency preferences, and installation considerations in residential settings.</p></p>

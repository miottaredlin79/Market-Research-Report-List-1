<p><p>The extreme low and cryogenic sensor market includes various types designed for precise temperature measurement in challenging conditions. Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) sensors exhibit resistance that decreases with temperature, making them ideal for low-temperature environments. Resistance Temperature Detectors (RTDs) provide high accuracy and stability through a predictable change in resistance with temperature. Thermocouple sensors function across a wide temperature range by generating a voltage proportional to temperature difference. Other sensors may include semiconductor and optical sensors, each with unique applications in cryogenic settings.</p></p>

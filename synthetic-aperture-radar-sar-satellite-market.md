<p><p>Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) Satellites are categorized into high orbit and low orbit markets based on their operational altitude and capabilities. High orbit SAR satellites, typically stationed at geostationary orbits, provide broad coverage and continuous monitoring, making them suitable for large-scale environmental and military applications. In contrast, low orbit SAR satellites, positioned at altitudes below 2,000 kilometers, offer higher resolution imagery and rapid revisit times, making them ideal for detailed land observation, disaster management, and urban planning tasks.</p></p>

<p><p>The lower extremity prostheses market comprises various types tailored to different amputee needs. Above-knee prostheses, used for transfemoral amputations, offer enhanced mobility and stability. Below-knee prostheses cater to transtibial amputations, promoting natural gait. Ankle prostheses assist with foot rehabilitation and restoration of function, while hip disarticulation prostheses serve those with complete loss of the lower limb at the hip joint. Each type addresses specific anatomical requirements, functionality, and lifestyle considerations for amputees.</p></p>

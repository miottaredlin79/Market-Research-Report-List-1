<p><p>Ischemia Reperfusion Injury (IRI) occurs when blood supply returns to tissue after a period of ischemia or lack of oxygen. This restoration, while crucial for survival, can paradoxically lead to further tissue damage due to inflammatory responses and oxidative stress. IRI is particularly significant in contexts such as organ transplantation, cardiovascular surgeries, and stroke. </p><p>The Ischemia Reperfusion Injury Market is experiencing robust growth, driven by advancements in medical research, increasing incidence of cardiovascular diseases, and a rising number of organ transplants. Emerging therapeutic strategies, including pharmacological interventions and innovative surgical techniques aimed at mitigating IRI, are also propelling market expansion.

<p><p>A Hall effect speed sensor is a type of transducer that detects the speed and rotational position of objects, primarily used in automotive and industrial applications. It operates on the principle of the Hall effect, where a magnetic field influences the movement of charge carriers, generating a voltage that correlates with the speed of a rotating component. These sensors are favored for their accuracy, durability, and low power consumption, making them ideal for various applications, including vehicle speed measurement, wheel speed detection, and motor control.</p><p>The Hall effect speed sensor market is witnessing significant growth driven by the rising demand for advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) and electric vehicles.
